Jon Kimmich Fireside Chat

Worked at Microsoft, did a bunch of stuff. Worked at other places too.

To make your product, you need to either:

  1. Use your own money.
  2. Use OPM (Other Peoples Money)

When you use OPM, there will be people who will tell you what to do.

Entertainment tends to be risky, investors are hesitant.

Publishers are good at spending money well. They know what will and wont work. "Smart Money". A good option for starting.

Lots of emphasis on releasing many games to learn what works and what doesnt.

Dont ping pong between different kinds of games. Build up knowledge of how to make a really great game of a specific genre. eg. Bungie is all about FPS games.

Angels are mysterious creatures. You never know where youll find them. Dont bet on them for your business. Opportunistic and unpredictable.

Size of the community starts with matters when you start crowdfunding. Think: How much do I expect from every person? Therefore, how many people do I need?

Hard to get work done during crowdfunding because it is emotionally draining and requires engagement with the community. (Also, stuff like backer rewards.)

Making games is not the same as raising money. You need someone on your team who is passionate about raising money, or it wont happen.

Financial Implications

  • How many impressions do you have?
  • How many installs per impression?
  • How many purchases per install?
  • Average revenue per unit?
  • Average time played?

Be aware of grants. Apparently there exists a very easy interface for grants in BC. Just check boxes and it figurs it out for you.
